ENS
What is ENS

The National Security Scheme:
Spain's mandatory security framework.

The Esquema Nacional de Seguridad establishes the principles and minimum information security requirements that must be met by the information systems of Spanish Public Administrations and their suppliers.

Approved by Royal Decree 311/2022, it updates the previous RD 3/2010 and aligns with the eIDAS Regulation and the NIS2 Directive. It is not a recommendation: it is a legal requirement with direct consequences on your ability to contract with the public sector. Discover also how ENS becomes the foundation for ISO 27001, ISO 22301 and NIS2.

Legal framework
Royal Decree 311/2022, of 3 May (BOE-A-2022-7191). Repeals RD 3/2010 and applies immediately.
Scope of application
Public Administrations and their public sector entities. Providers of information systems, cloud services, software and data for the public administration.
ENS Categories

Which ENS certification category do you need?

Basic Category
45 measures
For systems with limited impact. Affecting a small number of citizens or low-value assets. Applicable to most SME ICT suppliers to the public sector.
Medium Category
60 measures
For systems with significant impact. Affects a considerable number of citizens or medium-value assets. Requires two-factor authentication and periodic monitoring.
High Category
75 measures
For critical infrastructures and essential services. Affects more than 100,000 citizens. Requires digital certificate, SIEM and generator with 2-hour autonomy.

An important distinction

Declaration of conformity vs
ENS certificate: they are not the same.

RD 311/2022 provides two ways to demonstrate ENS compliance, and many companies do not know the difference until it is too late.

Declaration of Conformity

It is a self-declaration by the supplier itself. The company declares that it meets the ENS requirements without an external body verifying it. It is only valid for the Basic category and carries less weight in tender specifications.

  • Only valid for ENS Basic
  • Does not require an external audit
  • Less credibility with public bodies
  • Many tender specifications explicitly reject it

ENS Certificate ❖

Issued by an ENAC-accredited certification body after an independent audit. It is the standard required by most relevant tender specifications and the only one that provides full legal certainty.

  • Valid for Basic, Medium and High
  • Verified by an accredited external body
  • Accepted in all tender specifications
  • The only one recognised in high-criticality contracts

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know
about ENS certification.

What exactly is the ENS?
The Esquema Nacional de Seguridad (ENS) is a Spanish regulatory framework established by RD 311/2022 that defines the minimum security requirements for the information systems of public bodies and their suppliers. In the Spanish public sector, it is the equivalent of what ISO 27001 is in the private market.
Does my company necessarily need the ENS?
Yes, if you provide ICT, cloud, software or information management services to any Spanish public body. Since 2022 it is an eliminating requirement in most public procurement specifications. Without it, your bid is not even evaluated.
What is the difference between Basic, Medium and High category?
The category depends on the potential impact of a security incident. Basic affects few citizens or low-value assets (45 measures). Medium affects a significant number or important assets (60 measures, requires 2FA). High affects critical infrastructures or more than 100,000 citizens (75 measures, requires SIEM and digital certificate). Your exact category is determined in the free diagnosis.
How long does ENS certification take?
With Avson, between 3 and 4 months for Basic or Medium category. The process includes: diagnosis (week 1), implementation plan (week 2), implementation and documentation (weeks 3–10) and certification audit (weeks 11–14). For High category, between 4 and 6 months.
